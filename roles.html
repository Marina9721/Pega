Roles in Pega

  • Citizen developer : non-technical business user who participates in the application development. Brings valuable knowledge about business needs and may perform the role of SME on a small Pega project.
  • System architect (SA) : designs and configures the app. Led by a Lead SA (LSA) : design the overall architecture. Senior SA (SSA) configure interface froms and automated decisions. Object-oriented design and technical implementation skills. Advocate for business users.
  • Business architect (BA) : works with SME and stakeholders to understand business needs. Defines business rules, SLA and processes.
  • Subject matter expert (SME) : has deep understanding of a particular business domain. Works with the project team to convey (transmit) business needs and helps validate information accuracy.
  • Scrum master : ensures all team members understand Scrum theory and practices. Facilitates Scrum events (daily meeting, sprint review...)
  • Product owner : owns the product backlog and prioritization of user stories. Creates acceptance criteria.
  • Quality assurance : creates and runs functional and performance test scripts, Scrum team.
  • System administrator and IT resources : provide experstise (infrastructure, security, integration...)
  • Specialty architects : engage with project development depending on project needs and skills alignment.
  • Consulting solutions executive (CSE) : provides leadership, oversees project delivery and direction, establishes best practices.
  • Project delivery leader : provides overall project plan and delivery guidance.

Personas and roles

  • Users are created to perform application tasks but they do not all perform the same tasks. (Service Representatives different from Client user). A user is created with an email address and a role.
  • Roles define the tasks a user can perform.
  • A Persona is the business representation of a role/type of user. Share common application security and assignments. A user is assigned a persona. When you create a new persona, Pega creates default permissions and a work queue for the persona.
Add a new persona : Users > User Management > Add Persona. Then assign it to users in People. Channels in Pega refer to the user interfaces that are used to perform tasks. The needs of different users require access to different application features. Channels are created before roles.
